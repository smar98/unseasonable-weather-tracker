How to read this

Why does unseasonable weather matter?

It is the timing, not just the heat or cold, that does the damage. A warm spell, cold snap or downpour that arrives out of season catches everything built around the normal calendar. This tool covers cities and towns; an out-of-season signal means:

  • Heat & health. Off-season heat hits people, hospitals and power grids before the summer routine — and its defences — are in place.
  • Water. When Himalayan snow falls or melts out of season, it shifts river flow and reservoir planning downstream.
  • Floods & hazards. Unseasonal cloudbursts drive urban flooding; in the hills, landslides and avalanches on slopes that aren't braced for them.
  • The land around them. Many grid cells take in nearby cropland, where off-season heat, cold or rain hits yields — a dedicated farm-region layer is planned, not yet built.

What a flag means

  • Temperature: the day sat beyond the seasonal 90th/95th (warm) or 10th/5th (cold) percentile of ~150 comparable baseline days — for daytime highs and nighttime lows both.
  • Heavy rain uses wet days (≥1 mm) only — the WMO convention.
  • Snow flags are amount-aware: rare-season occurrence, or an amount beyond the seasonal 95th percentile.

Base rates are stated

  • By construction ~10% of days breach a 90th percentile even under a stable 1991–2020 climate. The signal is the excess above that, not the count itself.
  • Rising warm exceedance largely reflects warming since the baseline — that is the finding, not a defect.

What this cannot say

  • Whether climate change caused a specific event (attribution needs a different design).
  • What an official IMD station recorded — ERA5 is a reanalysis estimate.
  • Conditions across a whole district — each city is one grid cell.

Cross-checks & validation

Method & AI-use note

  • Method. ERA5/ERA5-Land daily values compared to the same season across 1991–2020 with ETCCDI percentile indices (leave-one-year-out inside the baseline). Cross-checked against NOAA station temperatures and IMD gauge rainfall; a sample is hand-checked against news/IMD. Fully reproducible from the code.
  • AI use. Built by the author with an AI coding assistant (Claude) pair-programming the pipeline, charts and copy. Every threshold and claim was author-reviewed; the automated cross-checks exist so results rest on data, not on anyone's say-so.
