How to read this

Seasonal anomaly screening — not climate attribution Reanalysis estimates — not station observations Grid-cell values — not whole districts Every flag needs validation before public claims

What a flag means

  • Heat/cold flags: the day sat beyond the 95th/5th percentile of ~150 comparable baseline days (same season, 1991–2020).
  • Heavy precipitation uses wet days (≥1 mm) only — the WMO convention.
  • Snow flags are amount-aware: rare-season occurrence, or an amount beyond the seasonal 95th percentile of baseline snow days.

Base rates are stated

  • By construction ~10% of days breach a 90th percentile under a 1991–2020 climate. The signal is the excess above that, not the count itself.
  • Rising heat exceedance largely reflects warming since the baseline — that is the finding, not a defect.

What this cannot say

  • Whether climate change caused a specific event (attribution needs a different design).
  • What an official IMD station recorded — ERA5 is a reanalysis estimate.
  • Conditions across a whole district — each city is one grid cell.
